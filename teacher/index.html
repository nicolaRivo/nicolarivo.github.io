About Me

Hi, I'm Nicola

Born and raised in Milan, I took my first steps in music as a self-taught guitarist, performing in the Milanese folk scene. At 25 I moved to London to study piano, composition, and music production at the London Centre of Contemporary Music, graduating in 2020.

After five years immersed in London's vibrant music scene, I moved to Edinburgh for a Master's in Sound Design — expanding into audio technology, generative composition, and orchestral arrangement. Now back in London, I work as a teacher, performer, sound designer, and audio engineer.

I believe music lessons should feel joyful and personal. Every student has their own way of learning — my job is to find what clicks for you and make the journey genuinely fun.

How I Teach

My Approach to Music Lessons

Four principles that guide every lesson I teach — whether you're 6 or 60, a beginner or advanced.

01

Personalised Curriculum

Every student gets a plan built around their interests, learning style, and goals. I regularly adjust my approach to keep things engaging.

02

Theory Through Playing

I teach the practical side of music theory through application and creativity — with minimal emphasis on sight reading unless you want it.

03

Creativity First

My teaching centres on interpretation, improvisation, and understanding the mechanics of music. You'll learn to truly connect with your instrument.

04

No Pressure, Just Music

Learning happens best when you feel comfortable. I create a warm, supportive space where you can take risks and find your musical voice.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need my own instrument? +

Yes, having your own instrument is essential for practising between lessons. For beginners, I can help guide you on what to buy or rent. If you take lessons at my studio, you'll use my instruments during the session.

How often should I take lessons? +

Weekly lessons work best for most students — they keep the momentum going and ensure steady progress. Some advanced students benefit from twice-weekly sessions, while bi-weekly can work for highly motivated adults.

What's your cancellation policy? +

If you need to cancel, please give at least 24 hours' notice. Late cancellations (under 24 hours) will be rescheduled within the same week if possible. I'll always try my best to find an alternative time, but if we can't arrange a new slot within 7 days of the cancelled lesson, it will be forfeited. The more notice you give, the easier it is to be flexible!

Do you offer online lessons? +

Yes! I offer high-quality online lessons using video conferencing with optimised audio settings. Many of my students learn this way very effectively, and I provide resources designed specifically for the online format.

What ages do you teach? +

I teach students of all ages — from children around 5-6 years old through to adults of any age. My approach adapts to each student's developmental stage, learning style, and goals.

Do I need to learn to read music? +

Not at all. My approach emphasises practical skills, ear training, and understanding music through playing. If you do want to learn notation, I have special techniques that make it much easier and more intuitive than traditional methods.

How much should I practise between lessons? +

Consistency matters more than marathon sessions. For young beginners, 15-20 minutes daily is great. Intermediate students should aim for 30-45 minutes, and advanced students typically need 1-2 hours. I'll give you specific practice strategies tailored to your level.

What are Premium benefits? +

When you purchase 4 or more lessons in advance, you become a Premium student. This saves you £5 per lesson and unlocks WhatsApp support between lessons — you can send me questions anytime. You'll also get ad hoc video tutorials and personalised homework recordings to guide your practice between sessions.
